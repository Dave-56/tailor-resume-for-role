# Examples Reference

Use these examples to anchor positioning and gap-handling choices.

## Example 1: Growth Product Manager

Target signals:
- Activation and onboarding
- Experimentation
- Funnel analysis
- Cross-functional leadership

Good narrative choice:
- Position the candidate as a growth-focused PM
- Lead with onboarding and experimentation wins
- Mention SQL only to the level supported by the source material

Representative rewrite pattern:
- Led onboarding revamp across web and in-app flows, increasing week-1 activation by 12% by simplifying setup and clarifying value moments.
- Shipped 15+ A/B experiments across signup and trial, improving step-through rates and tightening experiment review with Analytics.

Key lesson:
- Deprioritize unrelated product work so the role fit is obvious quickly.

## Example 2: Customer Support / Success

Target signals:
- Backlog reduction
- CSAT or NPS improvement
- Escalation workflows
- Knowledge-base ownership

Good narrative choice:
- Position the candidate as a lead-ready IC or support-ops-heavy profile when they lack formal people management
- Show process leadership without changing the title

Representative rewrite pattern:
- Reduced median first response time by redesigning queues, introducing macros, and clarifying escalation paths while maintaining strong CSAT.
- Authored and standardized knowledge-base content to cut repetitive tickets and improve self-serve support.

Key lesson:
- When leadership depth is limited, showcase initiative, systems improvement, and cross-functional influence instead of inventing management scope.
