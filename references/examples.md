# Examples Reference

Use these examples to anchor positioning and gap-handling choices.

## Example 1: Growth Product Manager

Target signals:
- Activation and onboarding
- Experimentation
- Funnel analysis
- Cross-functional leadership

Good narrative choice:
- Position the candidate as a growth-focused PM
- Lead with onboarding and experimentation wins
- Mention SQL only to the level supported by the source material

Representative rewrite pattern:
- Led onboarding revamp across web and in-app flows, increasing week-1 activation by 12% by simplifying setup and clarifying value moments.
- Shipped 15+ A/B experiments across signup and trial, improving step-through rates and tightening experiment review with Analytics.

Key lesson:
- Deprioritize unrelated product work so the role fit is obvious quickly.

## Example 2: Customer Support / Success

Target signals:
- Backlog reduction
- CSAT or NPS improvement
- Escalation workflows
- Knowledge-base ownership

Good narrative choice:
- Position the candidate as a lead-ready IC or support-ops-heavy profile when they lack formal people management
- Show process leadership without changing the title

Representative rewrite pattern:
- Reduced median first response time by redesigning queues, introducing macros, and clarifying escalation paths while maintaining strong CSAT.
- Authored and standardized knowledge-base content to cut repetitive tickets and improve self-serve support.

Key lesson:
- When leadership depth is limited, showcase initiative, systems improvement, and cross-functional influence instead of inventing management scope.

## Evidence quality ladder

Use this ladder for the top 3-5 anchor bullets that need to carry the target role.

- `outcome-backed`: "Redesigned onboarding flows, increasing activation by 12%."
- `decision-backed`: "Prioritized self-serve setup work based on support trends, shifting the roadmap toward the highest-friction onboarding gaps."
- `scope-backed`: "Owned onboarding documentation and setup workflows used by partner teams across multiple launches."
- `responsibility-only`: "Managed onboarding documentation and worked with partner teams."

## Weak-to-strong transformations

Product / PM:
- Weak: "Wrote PRDs for new features."
- Stronger outcome-backed: "Defined requirements for onboarding improvements that reduced setup friction and helped lift activation."
- Stronger decision-backed: "Set requirements and trade-offs for onboarding work, aligning design and engineering on the highest-friction user flows first."

Customer success / support:
- Weak: "Worked with customers to resolve issues."
- Stronger outcome-backed: "Used customer issue patterns to improve escalation paths, reducing repeat tickets and making support handoffs smoother."
- Stronger decision-backed: "Synthesized customer feedback into recurring product and process fixes, helping the team prioritize the most common sources of friction."

Operations / admin:
- Weak: "Created guides for new hires."
- Stronger outcome-backed: "Standardized onboarding guides that reduced setup confusion and made ramp-up more consistent for new hires."
- Stronger scope-backed: "Built and maintained onboarding guides used across the team to keep setup steps and expectations consistent."

Design:
- Weak: "Collaborated with product and engineering on designs."
- Stronger outcome-backed: "Partnered with product and engineering to simplify key flows, improving usability and reducing avoidable user friction."
- Stronger decision-backed: "Used research and cross-functional input to shape design decisions on high-priority flows, clarifying what to simplify first."

Key lesson:
- Do not force every bullet into a metric. When no metric exists, strengthen it with decision quality, scope, or honest qualitative impact instead of leaving it as a task list.
